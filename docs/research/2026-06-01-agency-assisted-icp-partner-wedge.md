## Bounded question

Should Orvo actively target Tiendanube stores that already have an ecommerce agency/freelancer or app-heavy operating stack, and if so, how should the first offer avoid becoming a services/agency product?

## Short answer

Yes — **agency-assisted one-store merchants are a high-probability ICP segment**, but Orvo should sell to the merchant as the system of operational accountability, not to the agency as a broad portfolio dashboard yet. The agency/freelancer is a champion or resolver; the owner/operator remains the economic buyer until Orvo has enough multi-store workflow depth for a Scale/Custom agency product.

The strongest wedge is:

> “Tu agencia ejecuta; Orvo te avisa temprano qué necesita atención, con evidencia, estado y seguimiento por WhatsApp.”

## Why this ICP slice matters

Agency-assisted stores are often more mature than solo hobby sellers, but still suffer from the exact “who is watching today?” problem Orvo solves:

1. **More tools, more handoffs.** A store with Tiendanube + ERP/sync + shipping + Meta Ads + WhatsApp + freelancer/agency has more places where responsibility falls through the cracks.
2. **The owner still wants visibility.** Even when execution is outsourced, the owner asks for screenshots, updates, and “¿está todo bien?” messages.
3. **The agency creates case-resolver capacity.** Orvo is more valuable when someone can actually act on `stockout_risk`, `data_stale`, `fulfillment_backlog`, or `spend_without_orders`.
4. **Orvo can make the agency look better.** A daily evidence-backed queue helps the agency prove proactivity without building custom reports.

## Segment definition

### Best-fit agency-assisted ICP

| Signal | Strong fit | Weak fit |
|---|---|---|
| Store maturity | Tiendanube is active source of truth; paid plan; recurring order flow | Store is dormant or only experimental |
| Human workflow | Owner + agency/freelancer + ops/admin coordinate over WhatsApp | No one owns follow-up |
| Tool stack | Uses at least 2-3 apps/services: ERP, shipping, sync, ads, WhatsApp, spreadsheets | Only Tiendanube admin, no complexity |
| Pain | Owner asks for daily status; agency sends weekly/monthly reports too late | Owner only wants monthly analytics |
| Data hygiene | Stock/orders mostly maintained; willing to fix source issues | Tiendanube data known wrong and no one will fix it |
| Willingness to pay | Already pays agency/apps; accepts USD 79-199/mo for operational monitoring | Anchors only to free/very cheap apps |

### Buyer/champion/resolver map

| Role | Motivation | Orvo message |
|---|---|---|
| Owner/founder | Wants confidence without chasing people | “Todas las mañanas sabés qué necesita atención y quién debe mirarlo.” |
| Ecommerce/ops lead | Wants fewer manual checks and fewer forgotten issues | “Un queue diario de casos con evidencia, no más screenshots sueltos.” |
| Agency/freelancer | Wants to show proactivity and avoid blame for missed issues | “Orvo detecta temprano; vos resolvés y lo mostrás como gestión.” |
| Warehouse/admin | Needs simple, concrete tasks | “Stock/pedidos/datos stale en una lista accionable.” |

## What exists vs. what is missing

| Existing category | What it does well | Missing gap Orvo should own |
|---|---|---|
| Ecommerce agency/freelancer | Executes ads, uploads products, fixes campaigns, coordinates promotions | Does not continuously monitor cross-source operational state with case lifecycle |
| ERP/sync apps | Keep records, stock, invoices, channels aligned | Do not tell the owner which exception matters today across the full stack |
| Shipping apps | Labels, rates, tracking, carrier execution | Do not create owner-facing fulfillment aging cases tied to payment/order truth gates |
| WhatsApp CRM/chat tools | Manage customer conversations and broadcasts | Do not manage internal operational cases; WhatsApp is product, not projection |
| BI/reporting dashboards | Metrics and retrospective reporting | Pull-based; weak on daily action, follow-up memory, and degraded-data honesty |

## Packaging implications

### Keep the first agency-assisted offer merchant-led

Do **not** launch a broad agency dashboard first. Package as one-store Orvo Control with agency-friendly recipients/actions:

- **Starter / USD 79-99/mo:** 1 Tiendanube store, daily WhatsApp brief, up to 3 recipients, `data_stale`, `stockout_risk`, conservative configured cases.
- **Growth / USD 199/mo:** same store with more workflow depth, more recipients, weekly proof summary, verified fulfillment or ads cases when truth gates pass.
- **Scale / Custom later:** multi-store agency/client portfolio, account roles, cross-store summary, reseller billing, SLA. Do not sell this before operator workflow depth exists.

### Referral economics hypothesis

For early partner experiments, prefer a **simple referral credit or temporary revenue share** rather than a full reseller program:

- 10-20% referral share for 6-12 months, or one month credit per activated store.
- Merchant remains the customer of record.
- Orvo owns onboarding, truth gates, and support boundaries.
- Agency can be added as a WhatsApp/operator recipient only with merchant approval.

## Sales discovery questions for this segment

Ask these before demoing:

1. “¿Quién te avisa cada mañana si algo está trabado: agencia, equipo interno o nadie?”
2. “¿Qué te manda la agencia: reporte semanal/mensual, screenshots, o alertas cuando pasa algo?”
3. “Cuando hay bajo stock, pedido atrasado o caída de ventas, ¿quién se entera primero y por dónde?”
4. “¿Qué cosas terminás revisando vos aunque tengas agencia?”
5. “Si Orvo detecta un caso, ¿quién debería recibirlo por WhatsApp: vos, la agencia, operaciones, o todos?”
6. “¿Tiendanube es fuente confiable para stock/pedidos o hay otro sistema que manda?”

## Actionable GTM recommendations

1. **Add an agency-assisted ICP tag to qualification.** This should increase score only if there is a human resolver and Tiendanube data hygiene is acceptable.
2. **Turn the agency objection into a complement.** Use: “Perfecto, Orvo no reemplaza a la agencia; le da un queue temprano y auditable para actuar.”
3. **Sell per store first.** Avoid “20 stores under one cheap subscription” requests until Scale/Custom portfolio controls exist.
4. **Invite agencies as recipients, not owners of truth.** WhatsApp brief can go to owner + agency, but Operational Cases remain in Orvo’s governed lifecycle.
5. **Use weekly proof summaries as agency value evidence.** “Casos detectados, reconocidos, resueltos, reabiertos, datos stale” is more compelling than another performance report.
6. **Partner with point apps without competing.** Dux/Producteca/Envia-style tools can remain systems of record/execution; Orvo coordinates exceptions above them.

## Product guardrails

- Do not create agency-specific shortcuts that bypass compiled runtime, connector registry, run ledger, metric registry, Operational Cases, or audit.
- Do not let WhatsApp chat text become source of truth for case state.
- Do not promise fulfillment or ads cases to agency-assisted stores until connector truth gates pass.
- Do not position Orvo as “agency management software”; position it as the merchant’s operations control plane that agencies can act on.

## Bottom line

Agency-assisted Tiendanube merchants are attractive because they have both pain and capacity to act. The safe first move is **merchant-led, per-store Orvo Control with agency as a collaborator/recipient**. The agency channel can become a distribution loop, but the product should not become a portfolio/reseller platform until Orvo’s case workflow, permissions, and multi-store audit surfaces are ready.
